### 1. [[20_Research/Papers/Simulation/A Material Point Method for Thin Shells with Frictional Contact|A Material Point Method for Thin Shells with Frictional Contact]] `score 21.9` · TOG 2018
*Qi Guo, Xuchen Han, Chuyuan Fu, Theodore Gast, Rasmus Tamstorf, Joseph Teran*
**Tag:** `10.1145/3197517.3201346` · `/paper-analyze-graphics 10.1145/3197517.3201346`
Treats [[thin shell]] [[contact]] and friction as continuum elastoplasticity within [[MPM]], eliminating collision detection entirely. Decomposes shell kinematics into Kirchhoff-Love bending + normal shear/compression, resolving frictional contact through a yield surface. Handles 100K–1M DOF shell-contact scenarios in minutes per frame and naturally couples with granular [[MPM]] materials.

### 2. [[20_Research/Papers/Simulation/Incremental Potential Contact|Incremental Potential Contact]] `score 21.4` · TOG 2020
*Minchen Li, Zachary Ferguson, Teseo Schneider, Timothy R. Langlois, Denis Zorin, Daniele Panozzo, Chenfanfu Jiang, Danny M. Kaufman*
**Tag:** `10.1145/3386569.3392425` · `/paper-analyze-graphics 10.1145/3386569.3392425`
The foundational [[IPC]] paper. Formulates [[contact]] as a smooth barrier energy in the variational [[time integration]] objective, with CCD-filtered line search guaranteeing intersection-free trajectories at every solver iterate. Provides decoupled accuracy controls for dynamics and geometric conformity. Extensively benchmarked against commercial and research codes (Abaqus, ARCSim, SOFA). 217 citations.

### 3. [[20_Research/Papers/Simulation/A Massively Parallel and Scalable Multi-GPU Material Point Method|A Massively Parallel and Scalable Multi-GPU MPM]] `score 20.9` · TOG 2020

AoSoA data structure enables coalesced access without atomics. 100× speedup over CPU MPM, near-perfect scaling to 8 GPUs, 134M particles at <1 min/frame on 1024³ grid.

## Simulation & Physics-Based Animation

- **Reduced Immersed Method** (`10.1145/3355089.3356496`) — Real-time two-way coupled [[fluid simulation]] + [[deformable body]] via immersed boundary discretization with reduced-order elasticity. Twin subspaces for efficient Lagrangian-Eulerian transfer. Brandt et al. 2019.
- **Differentiable Solver with Contact** (`10.1145/3657648`) — Adjoint-based differentiable [[FEM]] + [[IPC]] for shape design, material estimation, and initial condition optimization. <10% overhead over forward sim. Huang et al. 2024.
- **MPM Lite** (`arxiv:2602.07853`) — Eliminates particles from the solve loop: resamples state onto fixed quadrature points via linear kernels, making solver cost independent of particle count. Enables off-the-shelf nonlinear solvers. Feng et al. 2026.
- **Dynamic Duo of FEM and MPM** (`10.1145/3641519.3657449`) — IMEX coupling of implicit [[FEM]] (cloth, flesh) with explicit [[MPM]] (fluid, granular) using [[IPC]] for frictional contact at the interface. Different timestep sizes coexist. Li et al. 2024.
- **Codimensional IPC** (`10.1145/3450626.3459767`) — Extends [[IPC]] to mixed codimension-0/1/2/3 with thickness offsets, C² strain limiting, and additive CCD. Minchen Li et al. 2021.
- **Fast But Accurate** (`10.1145/3730834`) — Real-time GPU implicit hyperelastic sim with nonlinear complementarity for [[contact]] and friction in a local-global framework. Zeng et al. 2025.
- **Implicit SPH Snow** (`10.1145/3386569.3392431`) — [[SPH]]-based snow with implicit compressible pressure solve + implicit shear. Handles accumulation, hardening, and two-way rigid body coupling. Gissler et al. 2020.
- **MPM for Magnetized Materials** (`10.1145/3478513.3480541`) — Langevin nonlinear paramagnetism coupled with MLS-[[MPM]] for magnetic deformable bodies and fluids. Sun et al. 2021.

## Vulkan Ray Tracing & Real-Time Rendering

- **ReSTIR** (`10.1145/3386569.3392481`) — [[ReSTIR]] reservoir resampling for real-time direct lighting from millions of dynamic lights. 6–60× faster than prior methods. Renders 3.4M emissive triangles at <50ms/frame with 8 rays/pixel. Bitterli et al. 2020. Foundational for modern real-time [[Vulkan]] RT pipelines.
